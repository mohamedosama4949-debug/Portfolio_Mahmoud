Who I Am

Engineer who builds
real-world systems

I'm Mohamed Osama — a Mechatronics Engineering student at Delta University for Science and Technology. My passion lives at the intersection of hardware and software, where sensors talk to microcontrollers and machine learning runs on the edge of embedded devices.

My flagship project, the Smart Gloves for Sign Language Translation, combines wearable hardware (ESP32-S3, flex sensors, MPU6050 IMU), on-device ML models trained via Edge Impulse, and a real-time audio output pipeline — making communication more accessible for the deaf and mute community.

I'm actively seeking internship and training opportunities in industrial automation and embedded engineering — ready to contribute from day one.
